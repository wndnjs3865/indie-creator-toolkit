You're picking between [Notion](https://notion.so) and [Logseq](https://logseq.com). Both serve writing workflows. Here's how they actually differ.

## Side-by-side

| | Notion | Logseq |
|---|---|---|
| Free tier | Personal use, unlimited blocks | Free, open source |
| Starting price | Free | Free |
| Paid price | $10/mo | — |
| Platform | Web, Mac, Windows, iOS, Android | Mac, Windows, Linux, iOS, Android |
| Open source | No | Yes |

## Where Notion wins

- Generous free tier
- Huge template library
- Strong block editor

### Notion downsides

- Overkill for short docs
- Search can be slow on big workspaces

## Where Logseq wins

- 100% free + open source
- Outliner UX
- Privacy-first (local files)

### Logseq downsides

- Mobile app less polished
- Smaller plugin community than Obsidian

## Verdict

- **Pick [Notion](https://notion.so)** if you value: solo creators wanting an all-in-one workspace, writers who like database-driven docs.
- **Pick [Logseq](https://logseq.com)** if you value: outliner-style note-taking, open-source Roam alternatives.

If you're starting from zero, the free tier of either lets you ship your first project before you commit. That's the right way to choose.
