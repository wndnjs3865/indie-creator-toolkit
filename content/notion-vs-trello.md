You're picking between [Notion](https://notion.so) and [Trello](https://trello.com). Both have generous free tiers. Both serve solo creators. But they aim at different problems — Notion is an all-in-one workspace built around databases and docs, Trello is pure kanban with zero ceremony. Here's how to pick.

## How to choose

- **Pick [Notion](https://notion.so)** if your project has docs, references, and tasks all interleaved — and you want them in one workspace with cross-linking.
- **Pick [Trello](https://trello.com)** if your project is "a list of cards moving across columns", you have non-technical collaborators, and you want zero configuration.

## The math

Both have meaningful free tiers. At small scale, neither costs anything. At paid tiers, Trello ($5/user/mo) is half Notion's price ($10/user/mo) — but the value comparison only makes sense if you're getting equivalent functionality, and they're aimed at different problems.

The right way to think about it: if you'd use Notion's database + doc + wiki layer, the $10 is a steal. If you'd use 5% of Notion and live in kanban, Trello at $5 is honest pricing.

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| Want tasks + docs + wiki in one place | **Notion** |
| Want pure kanban with zero ceremony | **Trello** |
| Have non-technical collaborators | **Trello** |
| Solo + complex project (research, drafts, tasks) | **Notion** |
| Mobile-heavy workflow | **Trello** |
| Need calendar / timeline natively | **Notion** |

If you're between the two — start free on Trello. If by the third project you find yourself wanting "this card to link to that doc to reference this database row", you've outgrown Trello and Notion is the move.
