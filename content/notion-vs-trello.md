You're picking between [Notion](https://notion.so) and [Trello](https://trello.com). Both serve project mgmt workflows. Here's how they actually differ.

## Side-by-side

| | Notion | Trello |
|---|---|---|
| Free tier | Personal use, unlimited blocks | Free: unlimited cards, 10 boards/workspace |
| Starting price | Free | Free |
| Paid price | $10/mo | $5/mo |
| Platform | Web, Mac, Windows, iOS, Android | Web, Mac, Windows, iOS, Android |
| Open source | No | No |

## Where Notion wins

- Generous free tier
- Huge template library
- Strong block editor

### Notion downsides

- Overkill for short docs
- Search can be slow on big workspaces

## Where Trello wins

- Easiest kanban on the market
- Generous free tier

### Trello downsides

- Limited views beyond kanban
- Atlassian ownership = enterprise drift

## Verdict

- **Pick [Notion](https://notion.so)** if you value: solo creators wanting an all-in-one workspace, writers who like database-driven docs.
- **Pick [Trello](https://trello.com)** if you value: lightweight kanban for solo creators.

If you're starting from zero, the free tier of either lets you ship your first project before you commit. That's the right way to choose.
