## How to choose in under 60 seconds

- **You're starting from zero and want readers to find you** → [Substack](https://substack.com). Sign up, publish, ship.
- **You want to own your list and scale to thousands of subs** → [Kit](https://kit.com). Free up to 10k.
- **Growth via referrals + ad revenue is part of the plan** → [beehiiv](https://beehiiv.com).
- **You write technical content and love markdown** → [Buttondown](https://buttondown.com). Cheapest paid tier here.
- **You want full control + a blog on the same domain** → [Ghost](https://ghost.org). Self-host or managed.

## Why I lead with Substack

I get this question every time. Three reasons:

1. **Lowest cognitive overhead.** A new newsletter writer's biggest enemy is friction — every minute spent fighting the platform is a minute not writing the next issue. Substack's UX is built around "just write and publish".

2. **Free until you charge.** You don't pay Substack a cent until you're already earning. The 10% revenue share only kicks in on *paid* subscribers — meaning the platform's incentives are aligned with your growth.

3. **Built-in audience.** Recommendations, Notes, and the discovery feed mean readers find you without you running ads. Most platforms make you bring your own audience.

If you've already tried Substack and hit its ceiling — drop me a note about what you needed instead. I update this list every quarter based on real reader feedback.

## What I deliberately left out

- **Mailchimp** — pricing model punishes growth, deliverability complaints have piled up.
- **Revue** — sunset by Twitter/X in 2023. Don't sign up.
- **Medium** — not a newsletter platform; you don't own the relationship at all.
- **Mailerlite** — solid alternative to Kit if you specifically want cheaper transactional sends, but the editorial UX lags Kit/beehiiv noticeably.
- **Self-rolled SES + Postal** — only sane if you're a developer who genuinely enjoys email infrastructure. For everyone else: Ghost self-hosted is the better answer.
