## How to choose

- **Pick [Substack](https://substack.com)** if you're starting from zero, you write for a general audience, and you want the platform to help you find readers. The 10% cut is worth it at small scale for the discovery flywheel.
- **Pick [Kit](https://kit.com)** if you're building a list-owned newsletter as a long-term business — automation, sequences, and the 10k free tier matter more than discovery.

## The math

At 1,000 paid subs × $5/mo, Substack takes $500/mo (forever). Kit at the equivalent tier costs $0 (under 10k subs free) — that's a $6,000/year difference at the same audience size. Past 10k subs, Kit jumps to ~$29-49/mo, still dramatically below Substack's revenue share.

The trade-off: Substack delivers some of those subs *for you* via recommendations. Kit makes you bring 100% of the audience yourself. So the right answer depends on whether you have a list to import (or growth path) or you need help building one.

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| Starting from zero, no audience | **Substack** |
| Have an existing list / audience | **Kit** |
| Need automation / sequences | **Kit** |
| Want to keep 100% of paid revenue | **Kit** |
| Want recommendations to grow you | **Substack** |
| Already on Substack and growing | Stay until ~200 paid subs, then re-evaluate |
| Treating newsletter as a business | **Kit** |

If you're between the two — try Substack for 30 days, see if recommendations grow your list. If yes, stay until ~200 paid subs, then migrate to Kit (the 10% becomes real money). If recommendations don't grow you, Kit's free tier was the right call from day one.
