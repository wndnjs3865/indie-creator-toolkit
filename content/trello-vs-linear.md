You're picking between [Trello](https://trello.com) and [Linear](https://linear.app). Both serve project mgmt workflows. Here's how they actually differ.

## Side-by-side

| | Trello | Linear |
|---|---|---|
| Free tier | Free: unlimited cards, 10 boards/workspace | Free: up to 10 users, 250 issues |
| Starting price | Free | Free |
| Paid price | $5/mo | $8/mo |
| Platform | Web, Mac, Windows, iOS, Android | Web, Mac, Windows, iOS, Android |
| Open source | No | No |

## Where Trello wins

- Easiest kanban on the market
- Generous free tier

### Trello downsides

- Limited views beyond kanban
- Atlassian ownership = enterprise drift

## Where Linear wins

- Best-in-class UX
- Keyboard-first
- Generous free tier

### Linear downsides

- Less template/workflow flexibility than ClickUp
- Issue cap on free

## Verdict

- **Pick [Trello](https://trello.com)** if you value: lightweight kanban for solo creators.
- **Pick [Linear](https://linear.app)** if you value: small dev teams or solo devs who want polish.

If you're starting from zero, the free tier of either lets you ship your first project before you commit. That's the right way to choose.
