## How to choose

- **Pick [Aseprite](https://www.aseprite.org)** if pixel art is what you're making — for games, illustrations, or anything where the pixel grid is the medium.
- **Pick [Affinity Photo](https://affinity.serif.com)** for any non-pixel-art digital work — illustration, photo editing, design, layout.

## The math

Both are essentially free. Aseprite is $20 once (or compile from source). Affinity Photo is fully free since 2025. Cost isn't the deciding factor — specialty is.

The only reason to choose between them is the type of work. They genuinely don't overlap much: Aseprite's pixel grid + sprite export pipeline doesn't make sense in Affinity Photo, and Affinity Photo's RAW-photo workflow doesn't make sense in Aseprite.

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| Making pixel art for games | **Aseprite** |
| Making pixel art illustrations | **Aseprite** |
| Photoshop replacement workflow | **Affinity Photo** |
| iPad-based digital painting | **Affinity Photo** (or Procreate) |
| Photo editing + retouching | **Affinity Photo** |
| Mixed pixel + non-pixel work | Buy both — total cost is $20 |

If you're between the two — they're genuinely complementary tools, not alternatives. Most pixel artists eventually own both. Aseprite for sprites, Affinity for promotional art / press kit / store page graphics.
