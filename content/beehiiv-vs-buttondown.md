## How to choose

- **Pick [beehiiv](https://beehiiv.com)** if growth is the primary goal — referral mechanics, ad revenue, and growth analytics are native, not bolted on.
- **Pick [Buttondown](https://buttondown.com)** if you write technical content and value markdown composition more than growth tooling. $9/mo flat is dramatically cheaper than beehiiv's paid tiers.

## The math

At 5,000 subs (above beehiiv's free tier), beehiiv's paid plan starts at $43/mo. Buttondown's equivalent tier is roughly $19-29/mo. Past ~10k subs, the gap widens — beehiiv jumps to $84/mo+, Buttondown stays in the $50-70 range.

The trade-off: beehiiv's referral mechanics + ad marketplace can *generate* revenue that pays for the higher fees. Buttondown is pure cost-minimization with no built-in monetization beyond paid subscriptions.

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| Growth is the primary goal | **beehiiv** |
| Technical writer, markdown lover | **Buttondown** |
| You want native referral mechanics | **beehiiv** |
| You want the cheapest serious paid tier | **Buttondown** |
| You'll monetize via ads + sponsorships | **beehiiv** |
| You'll monetize via paid subs only | **Buttondown** |

If you're between the two — start free on beehiiv (2,500 subs is generous). If by 1,000 subs the referral/ad tooling isn't earning its keep, migrate to Buttondown. The reverse migration (Buttondown → beehiiv) gets harder past a few thousand subs.
