## How to choose

- **Pick [Kit](https://kit.com)** if you're building a list-owned newsletter as a long-term business — automation, sequences, and the 10k free tier matter more than $9/mo savings.
- **Pick [Buttondown](https://buttondown.com)** if you write technical content, you live in markdown editors, and you want the cheapest serious paid tier on the market.

## The math

At 1,000 subs, Kit is free; Buttondown is ~$9-19/mo. At 5,000 subs, Kit is free; Buttondown is ~$19-29/mo. At 10,000 subs, Kit *just* hits paid (~$33/mo); Buttondown is ~$50/mo.

Kit's free tier dominates pure economics until you cross ~10k subs. Past that, Buttondown is cheaper but you lose Kit's automation depth — which often pays for itself via better retention and upsells.

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| Treating newsletter as a business | **Kit** |
| Need automation / sequences / tagging | **Kit** |
| Under 10k subs and want free | **Kit** |
| Technical writer, markdown lover | **Buttondown** |
| Want cheapest serious paid tier | **Buttondown** |
| Have a small, dedicated list | **Buttondown** |

If you're between the two — start free on Kit (10k subs is plenty of runway). If by 1,000 subs you find yourself fighting Kit's editor and missing markdown, migrate to Buttondown. The economics of Kit's free tier are hard to beat for the first year of writing.
