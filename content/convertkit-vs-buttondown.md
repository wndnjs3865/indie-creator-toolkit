You're picking between [Kit (formerly ConvertKit)](https://kit.com) and [Buttondown](https://buttondown.com). Both serve newsletter workflows. Here's how they actually differ.

## Side-by-side

| | Kit (formerly ConvertKit) | Buttondown |
|---|---|---|
| Free tier | Up to 10,000 subscribers free | Up to 100 subscribers free |
| Starting price | Free | Free |
| Paid price | $25/mo | $9/mo |
| Platform | Web | Web |
| Open source | No | No |

## Where Kit (formerly ConvertKit) wins

- Generous free tier
- Strong automation
- You own your list

### Kit (formerly ConvertKit) downsides

- Pricing scales fast above 10k subs
- Email design tools basic

## Where Buttondown wins

- Markdown-first composition
- Cheapest paid tier of the lot
- Indie-developer friendly

### Buttondown downsides

- Tiny free tier
- Less polished than Substack/beehiiv

## Verdict

- **Pick [Kit (formerly ConvertKit)](https://kit.com)** if you value: serious newsletter operators with their own list.
- **Pick [Buttondown](https://buttondown.com)** if you value: technical writers who want plain markdown email.

If you're starting from zero, the free tier of either lets you ship your first project before you commit. That's the right way to choose.
