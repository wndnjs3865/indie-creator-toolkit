## How to choose in under 60 seconds

- **You have a laptop and want to start painting today** → [Krita](https://krita.org). Free, full-featured, ships now.
- **You draw on iPad with an Apple Pencil** → [Procreate](https://procreate.com). $13 once, done forever.
- **You're making pixel art for a game or print** → [Aseprite](https://www.aseprite.org).
- **You also do photo editing or vector work** → [Affinity Photo](https://affinity.serif.com). Free since Canva acquisition.

## Why I lead with Krita in 2026

Three reasons.

1. **Painting-first, not photo-first.** Most "free Photoshop alternatives" treat painting as a checkbox. Krita's brush engine, stabilizers, and dynamic settings are designed by illustrators for illustrators.

2. **Free with no asterisks.** No revenue cap, no upgrade prompt, no "unlock by subscribing". The KDE community ships Krita for the love of it, not the funnel.

3. **Animation built in.** Even if you only ever paint stills, having animation native means you can experiment with motion without learning a second tool.

If you've already tried Krita and hit its ceiling — drop me a note about what you needed instead. I update this list every quarter based on real reader feedback.

## What I deliberately left out

- **Adobe Photoshop / Fresco** — strong tools, wrong audience for an indie illustrator's runway. The $22.99/mo Creative Cloud tax is a no-go for solo work.
- **Clip Studio Paint** — excellent for comics/manga, but my opinionated take is Krita matches it for general illustration without the licensing complexity.
- **Sketchbook (Autodesk)** — was free, then paid, now in limbo. Not stable enough to recommend in 2026.
- **MediBang Paint** — solid free option, but Krita's brush engine and active development put it ahead.
