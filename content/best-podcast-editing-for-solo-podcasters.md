## How to choose in under 60 seconds

- **You value your time and don't mind $16/mo** → [Descript](https://descript.com). Text-based editing is the workflow upgrade.
- **You're broke or want offline-first** → [Audacity](https://www.audacityteam.org). Free, simple, works on anything.
- **You already have an editor and just need clean output** → [Auphonic](https://auphonic.com).
- **You're a music-DAW veteran or want full pro-tier control** → [REAPER](https://www.reaper.fm).

## Why I lead with Descript in 2026

Three reasons.

1. **Text-based editing is the genuine workflow upgrade.** Once you've cut filler words by selecting them in a transcript, the audio-only timeline feels archaic. The hour-per-episode time savings pay for the subscription within the first month.

2. **Studio Sound covers a multitude of recording sins.** If your room has echo, your mic is mid, or your guest's setup is rough, Studio Sound papers over it. For solo podcasters without a treated booth, this single feature is worth the $16.

3. **It scales into video.** When you decide to put the show on YouTube — and you will — Descript's video editing uses the same transcript workflow. No new tool to learn.

If you've already tried Descript and it didn't fit — drop me a note about what you needed instead. I update this list every quarter based on real reader feedback.

## What I deliberately left out

- **Adobe Audition** — strong tool, wrong audience. The $22.99/mo Creative Cloud tax doesn't make sense vs Descript at $16.
- **Hindenburg Pro** — beloved by radio/journalism producers, but Descript's transcript workflow has eaten its main selling point.
- **GarageBand** — fine for first episodes, but you outgrow it within a season. Skip the migration tax.
- **Riverside / SquadCast** — these are *recording* tools (remote interview), not editors. Worth their own comparison.
