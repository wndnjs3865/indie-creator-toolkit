[Notion](https://notion.so) costs $10/mo. If that's not in the budget yet, here are free options that get most of the job done.

## Quick comparison

| Tool | License | Platform | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[Obsidian](https://obsidian.md)** | Free (proprietary) | Mac, Windows, Linux, iOS, Android | local-first markdown workflow; research that depends on backlinks |
| **[Logseq](https://logseq.com)** | Open source | Mac, Windows, Linux, iOS, Android | outliner-style note-taking; open-source Roam alternatives |

## Each alternative in detail

### Obsidian

[Obsidian](https://obsidian.md) is free to use and serves the same workflow as Notion for most beginners use cases.

**Why it works as a Notion alternative**

- Files are plain markdown — no lock-in
- Strong plugin ecosystem
- Works offline

**Where it falls short of Notion**

If you need solo creators wanting an all-in-one workspace, you'll eventually outgrow this.

### Logseq

[Logseq](https://logseq.com) is open source and serves the same workflow as Notion for most beginners use cases.

**Why it works as a Notion alternative**

- 100% free + open source
- Outliner UX
- Privacy-first (local files)

**Where it falls short of Notion**

If you need solo creators wanting an all-in-one workspace, you'll eventually outgrow this.

## When you should just pay for Notion

If you're already making money from your work and Notion would save you 2+ hours/week, the math says pay for it. Free alternatives are right when you're validating an idea or learning the craft.
