## How to choose

- **Pick [Krita](https://krita.org)** if your primary output is illustrations or digital paintings, you want the best free brush engine, or you specifically need animation timeline support.
- **Pick [Affinity Photo](https://affinity.serif.com)** if you do mixed work (illustration + photo + layout), you want native iPad support, or you don't need Linux.

## The math

Both are free. The cost equation is moot. The decision is workflow-specific:

- **Painting depth**: Krita wins (brush engine, stabilizers, animation).
- **Workflow breadth**: Affinity wins (raster + vector + photo + publish).
- **Platform coverage**: depends on your needs (Krita has Linux/Android, Affinity has iPad).

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| Pure digital painting / illustration | **Krita** |
| Need animation timeline | **Krita** |
| Linux user | **Krita** (Affinity not available) |
| iPad-based painting | **Affinity Photo** (or Procreate) |
| Mixed illustration + photo work | **Affinity Photo** |
| Need vector tools too | **Affinity Photo** (or Designer) |
| Care about open-source insurance | **Krita** |

If you're between the two — they're complementary, not competitive. Many illustrators run both: Krita for painting, Affinity for mixing in photos / final layouts / press kit. Both are free, so installing both costs nothing.
