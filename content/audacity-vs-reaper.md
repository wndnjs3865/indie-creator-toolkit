## How to choose

- **Pick [Audacity](https://www.audacityteam.org)** if you're editing podcasts, voice-overs, or mono speech recordings. The minimal learning curve and zero cost are dominant for these workflows.
- **Pick [REAPER](https://www.reaper.fm)** if you're producing music, doing serious multitrack mixing, or working with MIDI / instruments. The pro DAW feature set is unmatched at the price.

## The math

Audacity is $0 forever. REAPER is $0 to $225 on the honor system, with the typical license at $60 once. Across a 10-year career, both cost essentially nothing — neither is a financial decision.

The decision is workflow fit. Audacity for speech, REAPER for music. The overlap is small: a podcaster who never touches music never needs REAPER, a music producer who never edits speech never needs Audacity.

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| Solo podcast editing | **Audacity** |
| Voice-over / narration | **Audacity** |
| Music production | **REAPER** |
| Multitrack mixing (8+ tracks) | **REAPER** |
| MIDI / virtual instruments | **REAPER** |
| Offline + lightweight | **Audacity** |
| Want deep scripting / automation | **REAPER** |

If you're between the two — start with Audacity (it's free, fast, and proves whether you need a pro DAW). If you find yourself fighting Audacity's multitrack or MIDI gaps within a month, REAPER's $60 honor-system license is the right next step.

## Honest disclosure

Audacity is free, open source, and earns no commissions. REAPER's pay-what-you-can model is publicly listed. We compare them because they're the right pick for different readers — full stop.
