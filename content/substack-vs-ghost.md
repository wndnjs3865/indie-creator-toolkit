## How to choose

- **Pick [Substack](https://substack.com)** if you're starting from zero and want help finding readers. The 10% cut is real money long-term, but at small scale it's worth it for the discovery flywheel.
- **Pick [Ghost](https://ghost.org)** if you already have an audience to bring (or you're technical enough to grow one), and you want a blog + newsletter on the same domain with no platform tax.

## The math

At 1,000 paid subs × $5/mo, Substack takes $500/mo of your revenue (forever). Ghost(Pro) at $18/mo costs you $18/mo flat. The crossover is somewhere around 200 paid subs × $5 — past that, Ghost is cheaper every month.

The trade-off: Substack delivers some of those subs *for you* via recommendations. Ghost makes you bring 100% of the audience yourself. So the right answer depends on whether you have a list to import or you need help building one.

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| Starting from zero, no audience | **Substack** |
| Have an existing list / mailing list | **Ghost** |
| Technical, want full control | **Ghost** |
| Want a blog on the same domain | **Ghost** |
| Need automation / sequences | Neither — use [Kit](https://kit.com) |
| Already on Substack and growing | Stay until ~200 paid subs, then re-evaluate |

If you're starting from zero, both have free tiers — try Substack for 30 days, see if your audience grows from recommendations. If yes, stay. If no, the 10% cut isn't earning its keep — migrate to Ghost.
