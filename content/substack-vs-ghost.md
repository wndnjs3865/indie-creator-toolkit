You're picking between [Substack](https://substack.com) and [Ghost](https://ghost.org). Both serve newsletter workflows. Here's how they actually differ.

## Side-by-side

| | Substack | Ghost |
|---|---|---|
| Free tier | Free to publish; 10% take on paid subs | Self-hosted free; managed from $9/mo |
| Starting price | Free | Free |
| Paid price | — | $9/mo |
| Platform | Web | Self-host or managed (Web) |
| Open source | No | Yes |

## Where Substack wins

- Built-in audience discovery
- Zero technical setup

### Substack downsides

- 10% of paid subs is steep at scale
- You don't own the platform

## Where Ghost wins

- Open source
- Built-in payments
- Owns blog + newsletter

### Ghost downsides

- Self-hosting requires DevOps
- Managed hosting is the usual real cost

## Verdict

- **Pick [Substack](https://substack.com)** if you value: zero-setup paid newsletter.
- **Pick [Ghost](https://ghost.org)** if you value: full control over email + blog on the same domain.

If you're starting from zero, the free tier of either lets you ship your first project before you commit. That's the right way to choose.
