You're picking between [Audacity](https://www.audacityteam.org) and [Descript](https://descript.com). Both can edit a podcast — but they live in different decades. Audacity is the free, open-source, waveform-based editor that's been the indie default for 20+ years. Descript is the modern AI-first tool that lets you edit audio by editing the transcript. Here's how to pick.

## How to choose

- **Pick [Audacity](https://www.audacityteam.org)** if budget is the binding constraint, you work offline often, or you specifically value open-source insurance. The minimal learning curve and zero cost are dominant for these conditions.
- **Pick [Descript](https://descript.com)** if you value your time over $16/mo, your recording setup isn't pristine, and you might branch into video later. The text-based editing is a genuine workflow upgrade.

## The math

Audacity is $0 forever. Descript is $16/mo Hobbyist ($192/yr). Across two years, $384 vs $0.

The honest comparison: if Descript saves you 2 hours/month on editing (it does, once you internalize the transcript workflow), at any meaningful hourly value of your time it pays itself back many times over. The math only flips if your time is worth less than ~$8/hr — which is below US/UK minimum wage.

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| Time-conscious solo podcaster | **Descript** |
| Budget genuinely zero | **Audacity** |
| Offline / travel-heavy workflow | **Audacity** |
| Imperfect recording room | **Descript** (Studio Sound) |
| Music production / multitrack mixing | Neither — use **REAPER** |
| Plan to add video / YouTube later | **Descript** |
| Care about open-source insurance | **Audacity** |

If you're between the two — try Audacity first (it's free). If by your third episode you find yourself spending hours on filler-word removal or fighting room echo, Descript's $16/mo will pay itself back fast.
