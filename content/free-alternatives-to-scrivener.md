## When you should just pay $60 for Scrivener

If you're writing books, screenplays, or anything 30,000+ words seriously, $60 once is genuinely cheap for the genre standard. The corkboard view + compile feature pay back the cost on the first draft. Free alternatives are right when you can't pay, you're learning before committing, or you specifically want open-source insurance.

## What to skip

- **Google Docs** — fine for collaboration, terrible archive at 50,000+ words.
- **Microsoft Word** — Scrivener costs less long-term once you factor Word's subscription tax.
- **Notion** — not built for long-form draft structure (database-first model).
- **iA Writer** — beautiful focus tool, but no structure layer for novels.
- **yWriter** — historically a free Scrivener alternative, but development pace has slowed; Manuskript is more active in 2026.
