## How to choose in under 60 seconds

- **You want one workspace for everything (calendar + sponsors + drafts)** → [Notion](https://notion.so).
- **You just want pure kanban for draft status** → [Trello](https://trello.com).
- **You write a tech newsletter and live in keyboard shortcuts** → [Linear](https://linear.app).

## Why I lead with Notion in 2026

Three reasons.

1. **Newsletter ops fit a database.** Editorial calendar, sponsor pipeline, draft archive, recurring issues — these are all variations of "tracked items with status, owner, date". Notion's database-with-views model fits the work better than pure kanban.

2. **Templates compound.** The Notion template ecosystem for newsletters is mature — sponsor outreach trackers, content briefs, growth dashboards. You don't build from scratch.

3. **The free tier doesn't sunset.** Personal use stays free with unlimited blocks. You only pay when you genuinely outgrow solo workflows.

If you've already tried Notion and it didn't fit — drop me a note about what you needed instead. I update this list every quarter based on real reader feedback.

## What I deliberately left out

- **Airtable** — better databases than Notion in places, but the doc layer is weaker. If you've already built workflows there, stay; otherwise Notion is the better default.
- **ClickUp** — more flexible than Notion, but the configuration surface kills the speed advantage for solo writers.
- **Asana** — designed around team task assignment, which most solo newsletter writers don't need.
- **Monday.com** — enterprise-pricing, enterprise-overhead. Wrong fit.
