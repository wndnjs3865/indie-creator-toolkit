## How to choose

- **Pick [Kit](https://kit.com)** if you're building a list-owned newsletter as a long-term business — automation, sequences, and the 10k free tier matter more than growth tooling.
- **Pick [beehiiv](https://beehiiv.com)** if growth is the primary goal — referral mechanics, ad revenue, and growth analytics are native, not bolted on.

## The math

At 1,000 subs: Kit is free; beehiiv is free.
At 5,000 subs: Kit is free; beehiiv is paid ($43/mo).
At 10,000 subs: Kit just hits paid (~$29-49/mo); beehiiv is paid (~$84/mo).

Kit's free tier dominates pure economics until you cross ~10k subs. beehiiv's higher fees can be offset by the referral + ad revenue native to the platform — a $43/mo plan that earns $200/mo in ads is dramatically better than a free plan earning $0.

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| Treating newsletter as a business | **Kit** |
| Growth is the primary goal | **beehiiv** |
| Need automation / sequences / tagging | **Kit** |
| Want native referral mechanics | **beehiiv** |
| Will monetize via ads + sponsorships | **beehiiv** |
| Will monetize via paid subs only | **Kit** |
| Under 2,500 subs (validation phase) | **Kit** (10k free) |

If you're between the two — start free on Kit (10k subs is plenty of runway). If by 1,000 subs you find yourself wanting referral mechanics or ad revenue, switch to beehiiv. The reverse migration (beehiiv → Kit) gets harder past a few thousand subs because of automation rebuild work.
