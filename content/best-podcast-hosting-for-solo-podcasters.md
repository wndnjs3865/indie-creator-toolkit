## How to choose in under 60 seconds

- **You're starting from zero and just want it to work** → [Buzzsprout](https://buzzsprout.com). Sign up, upload, ship.
- **You're broke and your audience lives on Spotify** → Spotify for Podcasters. Switch hosts later if you outgrow it.
- **You'll run more than one show** → Transistor.
- **You want to do live audio chats** → Podbean.
- **You care about monetization from day 1** → Captivate.
- **Your podcast is part of a WordPress site** → Castos.

## Why I lead with Buzzsprout

I get this question every time. Three reasons:

1. **Lowest cognitive overhead.** A new podcaster's biggest enemy is friction — every minute spent fighting the host is a minute not making the show. Buzzsprout's UX is built around "just upload and ship".

2. **You won't outgrow it for ~2 years.** Most solo podcasters never need the IAB v2 analytics that Transistor's pricing assumes. By the time you do, you've got episode revenue funding the move.

3. **Honest pricing.** Tiers based on monthly upload hours, not artificial feature paywalls. The $12/mo plan is genuinely complete for a weekly 30-minute show.

If you've already tried Buzzsprout and it didn't fit — drop me a note about what you needed instead. I update this list every quarter based on real reader feedback.

## What I deliberately left out

- **Anchor (legacy)** — folded into Spotify for Podcasters. Don't sign up for Anchor as a new account.
- **Libsyn / Blubrry** — solid hosts, but their UX hasn't aged well versus Buzzsprout. If you're starting today, skip them.
- **Riverside / Descript** — these are *recording/editing* tools that include hosting. If recording is your bottleneck, those are worth their own comparison ([see editing comparison](../audacity-vs-reaper)).
