You're picking between [Substack](https://substack.com) and [Buttondown](https://buttondown.com). Both serve newsletter workflows. Here's how they actually differ.

## Side-by-side

| | Substack | Buttondown |
|---|---|---|
| Free tier | Free to publish; 10% take on paid subs | Up to 100 subscribers free |
| Starting price | Free | Free |
| Paid price | — | $9/mo |
| Platform | Web | Web |
| Open source | No | No |

## Where Substack wins

- Built-in audience discovery
- Zero technical setup

### Substack downsides

- 10% of paid subs is steep at scale
- You don't own the platform

## Where Buttondown wins

- Markdown-first composition
- Cheapest paid tier of the lot
- Indie-developer friendly

### Buttondown downsides

- Tiny free tier
- Less polished than Substack/beehiiv

## Verdict

- **Pick [Substack](https://substack.com)** if you value: zero-setup paid newsletter.
- **Pick [Buttondown](https://buttondown.com)** if you value: technical writers who want plain markdown email.

If you're starting from zero, the free tier of either lets you ship your first project before you commit. That's the right way to choose.
