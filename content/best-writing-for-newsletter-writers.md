## How to choose in under 60 seconds

- **You want plain markdown drafts you own forever** → [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md). Free, local, zero lock-in.
- **You run your newsletter as part of a broader business** → [Notion](https://notion.so).
- **You research for weeks before drafting** → [Logseq](https://logseq.com). Outliner + daily journal.
- **You're writing 3,000+ word issues or a book on the side** → [Scrivener](https://www.literatureandlatte.com/scrivener).

## Why I lead with Obsidian in 2026

Three reasons.

1. **Your drafts outlive your tools.** Obsidian writes plain markdown to a folder on your machine. Five years from now, when this list looks dated, those files still open in any text editor on Earth. That portability is non-negotiable for a writer.

2. **Backlinks compound.** Once you've been writing for six months, the graph of "what I've already said about X" becomes a research asset. New issues start by surfacing relevant past notes — not by staring at a blank page.

3. **Paste-to-publish works.** Markdown drafts paste into Substack, Buttondown, Ghost, and beehiiv without reformatting. No "export step" between writing and publishing.

If you've already tried Obsidian and it didn't fit — drop me a note about what you needed instead. I update this list every quarter based on real reader feedback.

## What I deliberately left out

- **Google Docs** — fine for collaboration, terrible for archive. Once you have 200+ drafts, the file picker becomes the bottleneck.
- **iA Writer** — beautiful minimalist editor, but no archive/backlink layer. Use it as a focus surface alongside Obsidian if you like.
- **Bear** — gorgeous Mac/iOS app, but Apple-only and the markdown is slightly non-standard.
- **Roam Research** — invented the backlink workflow, but the $15/mo subscription doesn't compete with Obsidian's free.
