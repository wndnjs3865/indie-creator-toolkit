You're picking between [Krita](https://krita.org) and [Aseprite](https://www.aseprite.org). They're both excellent — but they live in different worlds. Krita is the open-source general digital painting tool with the best free brush engine. Aseprite is the genre-standard pixel art editor that game devs use to ship sprite sheets. The right pick depends entirely on whether you're making pixel art or anything else.

## How to choose

- **Pick [Krita](https://krita.org)** for general digital painting — illustrations, character art, concept work, anything where you're not working at the pixel grid level.
- **Pick [Aseprite](https://www.aseprite.org)** for pixel art — sprite sheets, retro game art, pixel-style illustrations. The genre tooling is unmatched.

## The math

Krita is $0, forever. Aseprite is $0 from source or $20 once for the binary. Neither is a financial decision — both are essentially free vs Adobe's $22.99/mo subscription.

The decision is medium. Pixel art is its own discipline; general illustration is its own discipline. They overlap maybe 5%.

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| General digital illustration | **Krita** |
| Pixel art for games | **Aseprite** |
| Character animation (high-res) | **Krita** |
| Sprite-sheet animation | **Aseprite** |
| Concept art / paintings | **Krita** |
| Retro game art | **Aseprite** |
| Mixed pixel + non-pixel work | Both — total cost is $0-20 |

If you're between the two — they're complementary tools, not alternatives. Many illustrators own both: Krita for painting, Aseprite for any pixel work. Combined cost is $0-20.
