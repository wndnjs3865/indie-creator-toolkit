You're picking between [Logseq](https://logseq.com) and [Scrivener](https://www.literatureandlatte.com/scrivener). They're at opposite ends of the writing-tool spectrum. Logseq is a free outliner-style note-taking tool built around bullet trees and bidirectional links. Scrivener is a $60 long-form writing powerhouse built around scenes, drafts, and compile-to-publish. Most serious writers eventually use both. Here's how to pick where to start.

## How to choose

- **Pick [Logseq](https://logseq.com)** if you're researching, journaling, or breaking stories with beat-sheet methodology. The outliner workflow + daily journal lower friction for capture-mode work.
- **Pick [Scrivener](https://www.literatureandlatte.com/scrivener)** if you're writing the actual long-form draft — book, screenplay, long essay. The scene/corkboard tooling and compile feature solve problems Logseq doesn't try to solve.

## The math

Logseq is $0 forever. Scrivener is $60 once. Total cost across a career: $60.

The decision isn't price — it's stage of the work. Logseq is for *before* you start drafting (research, idea capture, beat sheets). Scrivener is for *during* the draft (writing, restructuring, compiling). Many writers use both.

## Verdict

| Situation | Pick |
|---|---|
| Research / idea capture | **Logseq** |
| Daily journaling / beat sheets | **Logseq** |
| Writing a book / novel | **Scrivener** |
| Writing a screenplay | **Scrivener** |
| Long-form essay (5,000+ words) | **Scrivener** |
| Short-form (newsletter, blog post) | **Logseq** (or Obsidian) |
| Need .docx/.epub/.fdx export | **Scrivener** |
| Care about open-source insurance | **Logseq** |

If you're between the two — they're complementary, not competitive. Many serious writers use Logseq for research + capture, then move into Scrivener when the draft begins. Combined cost is $60 — cheap for the entire writing pipeline.
